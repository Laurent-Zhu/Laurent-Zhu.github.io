Yifan Zhu / 朱羿帆

I am a junior Software Engineering undergraduate at Northwestern Polytechnical University. My current research focuses on large language models, multimodal agents, AI agents, and intelligent 3D-CAD modeling.

I am especially interested in building systems that connect language, vision, executable CAD programs, and structured engineering knowledge.

I am contributing to BrepLLM, an ECCV 2026 provisionally accepted paper that studies how large language models understand boundary representations, and to a unified multimodal agent framework for CadQuery-based 3D-CAD generation, understanding, and editing.

In graduate study, I hope to systematically explore model architecture design, multimodal agents, and AI agents, and later work on frontier LLM research in leading technology companies or high-level research labs.

Unified Multimodal Agent Framework for CadQuery-based 3D-CAD Generation, Understanding and Editing

Ongoing research

This project studies a multimodal agent framework for 3D-CAD generation, understanding, and editing, using executable Python CadQuery programs as the unified modeling representation. My work focuses on high-quality CadQuery data construction, unified SFT data format design, model fine-tuning, and CAD-kernel-based automated evaluation and repair.

Dual-modal UAV Urban Traffic Perception and Intelligent Analysis System

Undergraduate Innovation Training Project

A UAV-based urban traffic object detection and visualization system using visible-light and infrared dual-modal fusion. The system targets parking lots, main roads, residential areas, and other urban scenes, trains a customized YOLOv8-OBB fusion model on DroneVehicle, and uses an Edge-Cloud-Dashboard architecture for 15 fps WebSocket streaming, FastAPI multi-node forwarding, and React-based real-time monitoring and offline replay.

AgriGuard Smart Agricultural Pest Management Platform

Agile Development Practice · Software Engineering Capstone Course

An end-to-end digital pest control system for farmers, managers, and field workers, integrating pest image recognition, RAG-based treatment recommendation, task scheduling, work-log traceability, and 7-day risk forecasting into a browser-accessible workflow. The stack uses Vue 3, Element Plus, ECharts, Django 5, DRF, JWT, YOLO, FAISS, LLM, and MySQL; our 7-person Scrum team delivered 3 sprints and 298+ backend automated test cases.
