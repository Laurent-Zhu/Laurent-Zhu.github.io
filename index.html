About

I am a third-year Software Engineering undergraduate at Northwestern Polytechnical University (NPU). My research interests lie at the intersection of large language models, CAD model generation, machine learning and natural language processing. I am currently contributing to two research projects: BrepLLM (under review at ECCV 2026) and a CAD code generation project (targeting NeurIPS 2026).

Outside of research, I enjoy building things end-to-end — from backend services and AI agent orchestration to small experiments that connect language models with structured engineering domains. I aim to pursue graduate studies that deepen my understanding of how language models can reason about structured, geometric and engineering knowledge.

Research

BrepLLM — Boundary Representation with LLMs

5th author · Under review at ECCV 2026

Exploring how large language models can understand and generate boundary representation (B-rep) data for solid CAD models. Contributing to dataset construction, model evaluation pipelines, and ablation experiments on geometry-aware tokenization.

LLM CAD Geometry PyTorch

CAD Code Generation with LLMs

In progress · Targeting NeurIPS 2026

Investigating LLM-driven generation of parametric CAD code (e.g. CadQuery / OpenSCAD) from natural-language descriptions, with a focus on structural validity, edit-ability and downstream geometric constraints.
